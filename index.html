Explore how climate technologies across the TIF and TGIF portfolios are forecast to alter the trajectory of global emissions over the next 15 years.

In this first-of-its-kind interactive experience, Capricorn Investment Group presents the global emissions landscape as it is today and how it could evolve through the scaling of climate technologies across the global economy.

Centered on the portfolios of the Technology Impact Fund (TIF) and Technology Growth Impact Fund (TGIF), this interactive experience builds upon the pioneering systems modeling work of Professor Bojana Bajželj* and incorporates updated public data to visualize relationships between economic activities and greenhouse gas emissions at planetary scale.

The purpose is to make climate impact more transparent, tangible, and decision-useful: showing where emissions come from, how systems are connected, and where innovation can shift the trajectory.

From there, users can explore three 2040 scenarios and examine how technologies across the TIF and TGIF portfolios are transforming critical systems and accelerating the transition to a lower-carbon economy.

Begin exploring the flows shaping our future.

In 2025, global emissions totaled 54GT of CO2e.

Transitioning to a low-carbon economy requires understanding the origin of these emissions.

The same total emissions can be viewed through seven different lenses.

These lenses include the final services provided to us, such as travel and food.

They also include the economic sectors that provide those final services, the equipment that composes each sector, the devices that make up equipment, the final energy that powers devices, the fuels we use, and the types of greenhouse gas emissions.

Each lens is inclusive of all the world's emissions. Emissions can be traced between domains as they flow through the global economy.

Each lens can be broken down into nodes, such as travel and food when looking through the lens of final services. Together, all the nodes for one lens sum to global emissions.

Flows between neighboring nodes show how emissions are connected across lenses, with flow width reflecting the magnitude of those connections. For example, of the 6 Gt CO2e due to Passenger Transport, 4.4 Gt are due to cars.

This is the landscape of global emissions in which Capricorn's Technology Impact Fund family is driving decarbonization.

The technologies in the TIF and TGIF portfolios have each been assigned to a node and, together, touch many parts of the global economy.

Select a company from TIF and TGIF's portfolio to see where its specific technology makes an impact.

Fervo

Fervo Energy is scaling next-generation geothermal power.

Geothermal power has a direct impact on electricity and heat emissions, where low-carbon baseload energy can help reduce dependence on fossil fuel generation across the economy.

 Propel Aero

Propel Aero is advancing hybrid electric air travel for regional transport.

Electric air travel technology has a direct impact on plane emissions, resulting in lower emissions for direct oil use and passenger transport when viewed through different lenses.

 Electric Hydrogen

Electric Hydrogen is scaling cost-competitive green hydrogen production.

Green hydrogen has a direct impact on chemical emissions, although future applications of the technology may impact emissions from passenger transport, freight, and industrial activities like iron and steel.

 Redwood Materials

Redwood Materials is advancing the circular economy through technologies for battery repurposing and recycling.

Battery repurposing has a direct impact on emissions from other industries, which includes battery manufacturing.
