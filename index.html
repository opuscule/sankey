Explore how climate technologies across the TIF and TGIF portfolios are forecast to alter the architecture of the global emissions landscapesystem over the next 15 years.

In this first-of-its-kind interactive experience, Capricorn Investment Group presents the global emissions landscapeworld as it is today and howas it could evolve through the scaling of climate technologies across the global economy.

Centered on the portfolios of the Technology Impact Fund (TIF) and Technology Growth Impact Fund (TGIF), the experience builds upon the pioneering systems modeling work of Professor Bojana Bajželj* and incorporates updated 2025 data to visualize the relationships between economic activitiesy and greenhouse gas emissions at planetary scale.

From there, users can explore possible 2040 futures and examine how technologies across the TIF and TGIF portfolios are transforming critical systems and accelerating the transition to a lower-carbon economy.

Global emissions are concentrated in a handful of tightly linked production systems, from industry and transport to food and buildings.

As pressure shifts across those systems, one central flow fragments into multiple pathways that each demand a distinct decarbonization strategy.

The result is a more distributed emissions architecture, where intervention points multiply and portfolio-level coordination becomes essential.
